DERMATOLOGY

Skin problems are not exclusive to humans; animals can also be affected. Just like us, pets should have regular check-ups with a dermatologist. They can suffer from skin rashes, allergies, infections, and other issues, especially in hot weather. Since animals spend a lot of time on the ground, their skin is more susceptible to infections. It's important to take skin conditions in pets seriously, as some diseases can be passed on to humans. Taking care of your pet's skin can also benefit the health of your entire household. Our veterinary dermatologists are experts in treating skin, hair, and ear conditions in pets.

Common signs of skin and coat health problems in pets include:

  • Excessive itching and scratching
  • Redness or inflammation of the skin
  • Hair Loss
  • Dry or Flaky Skin
  • Lumps or Bumps
  • Rashes
  • Odor from Skin
  • Licking, Biting, or Chewing Skin
  • Discoloration of Skin
  • Ulcers or Sores
  • Ear Problems
  • Changes in Nail or Paw Health

Skin and ear issues are frequently the cause for pets needing veterinary attention. Our veterinarians have the expertise to diagnose and treat various skin, ear, and nail problems using their medical knowledge and advanced testing methods to determine the most effective treatment for your pet.

Possible Causes of Dermatological Diseases in Pets

  • Allergies
  • Parasites (e.g., Fleas, Ticks)
  • Fungal Infections
  • Bacterial Infections
  • Hormonal Imbalances
  • Nutritional Deficiencies
  • Environmental Factors
  • Genetic Predispositions
  • Autoimmune Disorders
  • Stress or Anxiety-Related Issues
  • Contact with Irritants
  • Underlying Health Conditions

Our dermatology team is available to assist if a skin problem continues, worsens, or lacks a clear diagnosis, making it more challenging to handle. Dealing with chronic or seasonal itchy dermatitis can be incredibly frustrating for both pet and owner. Nevertheless, there are numerous treatment options currently accessible to enhance the overall health and quality of life.

FAQs

How can skin disease in dogs be treated?

When it comes to treating skin disease in dogs, the first step is to address the infection. Antibiotics are typically used to eliminate any bacteria that may be causing the issue, while antifungal medication can help prevent the growth of fungus. Additionally, antiseptic washes and corticosteroids may be utilized to reduce inflammation in the affected area. Deworming is also recommended as it has been shown to have a positive impact on cases of canine acne and other dermatological issues in animals.

For the best pet dermatology treatments in Dubai, consider seeking out dedicated professionals who offer top-quality care for your beloved furry companions.

How can I determine if my dog is suffering from a fungal or bacterial infection?

If you are unsure about whether your dog has a fungal infection, there are some signs to look out for:

  • White or yellowish-white patches on the skin
  • Thick, rough skin that resembles a piece of bark attached to the dog's body, which can be caused by fleas and ticks.

If you observe these symptoms in your pet and they do not improve within a few days, it is important to contact the veterinarian immediately. Fungal infections are common in dogs, but not all pets are affected by them. It can be challenging for owners to recognize when something is wrong, as there may not be any noticeable changes in their pet's appearance or behavior, and sometimes even their personality.

How did my dog contract a bacterial skin infection?

Bacterial skin infections in dogs are commonly caused by bites or scratches and can be transmitted through the air, contaminated water, or contact with other animals. If a bacterial infection has developed, it is crucial to prevent the spread of bacteria, alleviate pain, and promote healing in the affected area.

Furthermore, it is important to be aware of the necessary steps to take if your dog has been exposed to an infection before receiving treatment. This way, you can avoid subjecting your dog to unnecessary stressors, such as having an uncomfortable bandage on their face for an extended period until they recover.
